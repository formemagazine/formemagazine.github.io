FORME

Carolyn Bessette-Kennedy remains one of fashion’s most enduring minimalist icons. Known for her refined 1990s aesthetic — silk slip dresses, tailored black coats, sleek sunglasses, and understated beauty — she embodied a quiet confidence that continues to influence modern style. Born in 1966, she worked in public relations for Calvin Klein before stepping into global attention through her marriage to John F. Kennedy Jr. Despite intense media scrutiny, she maintained a private and composed presence. Her wardrobe was built on restraint: neutral palettes, clean lines, and precise tailoring. Her life was tragically cut short in 1999, yet her influence remains powerful. As 90s minimalism resurfaces today, a new generation is rediscovering her disciplined, timeless approach to style.
